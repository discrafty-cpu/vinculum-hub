Gathering Time: Nibi Learns from Nokomis

Nibi sits beside Nokomis in the early morning, watching how she gathers and sorts the natural materials that will become beautiful baskets. Everything must be counted, measured, and organized before the weaving can begin. Nibi learns that every number tells a story about the materials, and every pattern is a mathematical dance of light and dark, large and small.

The Preparation: Nibi Works with Strips

Now Nibi is ready to learn the real work of basket making. Nokomis teaches her how to prepare strips—cutting them to exact lengths, bundling them into groups, and planning how many of each color will be needed. This is where mathematics becomes the language of creation. Counting, adding, subtracting, and sharing—all are essential skills weavers must master.

Learning Mode:
Difficulty:

Production: Nibi Becomes a Weaver

Nibi has grown skilled. Now she takes on bigger projects—making multiple baskets, calculating materials for complex patterns, and planning her work week like Nokomis does. Every basket is a masterpiece that requires planning with numbers: adding and subtracting larger quantities, multiplying to find totals, and dividing to ensure every part is equal and balanced.
