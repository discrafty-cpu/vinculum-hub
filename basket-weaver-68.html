📊 Grade 6 — Problem 1
Makwa is now mastering the chemistry of dyeing fibers. A certain shade requires specific ratios of ingredients, and scaling up while preserving proportions is the key skill.
Makwa mixes dye using a ratio of 3 parts red powder to 5 parts water. If she uses 15 parts red powder, how many parts water does she need?
💡 Dye recipes must maintain exact ratios when scaled up for larger batches.
Grade 6 — Problem 2
Understanding material costs is crucial for running a successful basket business.
A fiber costs $0.35 per foot. How much do 20 feet of fiber cost?
💡 Calculating material costs is essential for basket business planning.
Grade 6 — Problem 3
Working with multiple colors requires careful balance to maintain traditional aesthetics.
A basket design uses fiber in the ratio 4:3:2 (natural:red:blue). If Makwa uses 8 pounds natural, how many pounds total does she need?
💡 Managing multiple fiber colors in set ratios maintains traditional aesthetics.
Grade 6 — Problem 4
Production rates determine what promises can be made for market day.
Makwa made 12 baskets in 4 hours. At this rate, how many baskets can she make in 10 hours?
💡 Production rates determine how many baskets to promise for market day.
Grade 6 — Problem 5
Pricing requires careful layered calculations.
Makwa's basket costs $8 in materials. She adds a 75% markup for her labor. Then the market charges a 10% selling fee on the final price. What does the customer pay?
💡 Pricing baskets requires layered percentage calculations.
Grade 6 — Problem 6
Temperature plays a critical role in the dye absorption process.
The temperature affects dye absorption. At 60°F, fibers absorb 2 oz of dye. At 80°F, they absorb 5 oz. At 100°F, they absorb 8 oz. What is the rate of change in absorption per degree?
💡 Understanding how temperature affects dye helps achieve consistent colors.
Grade 7 — Problem 1
Makwa explores how geometric transformations create the beautiful repeating patterns in traditional baskets.
A basket pattern motif is reflected across a vertical line of symmetry. If a point on the motif is at (3, 5), where is its reflection if the line of symmetry is x = 0?
💡 Symmetric basket patterns are created by reflecting motifs across a central axis.
Grade 7 — Problem 2
The circumference determines how much edging material is needed.
A circular basket has a diameter of 14 inches. What is its circumference? Use π ≈ 3.14.
💡 Knowing the circumference determines how much edging material is needed.
Grade 7 — Problem 3
Scaling designs while maintaining proportions is critical.
Makwa draws a basket design on graph paper. She wants to enlarge it by a scale factor of 2.5. If the original design is 4 inches wide and 6 inches tall, what are the new dimensions?
💡 Scaling basket designs up or down while maintaining proportions is a key skill.
Grade 7 — Problem 4
Right triangles appear frequently in basket design geometry.
A triangular basket section has sides of 5 cm, 12 cm, and 13 cm. Is this a right triangle? What is its area?
💡 Right triangles appear frequently in basket design geometry.
Grade 7 — Problem 5
Market research guides production decisions.
Makwa surveys 50 customers about favorite basket colors. Results: Brown 35%, Red 25%, Natural 20%, Blue 15%, Other 5%. If she makes 200 baskets next month, how many of each color should she make based on demand? (Answer for brown only.)
💡 Market research guides production decisions for basket makers.
Grade 7 — Problem 6
Calculating radius from area helps when designing lids.
A basket's circular top has area 154 cm². What is the radius? Use π ≈ 22/7.
💡 Calculating radius from area helps when designing lids to fit existing baskets.
Grade 8 — Problem 1
Makwa now models basket dimensions with equations and uses linear functions to predict material needs.
The cost C of making b baskets is given by C = 5b + 20 (where 20 is fixed overhead). If Makwa makes 12 baskets, what is her total cost?
💡 Linear cost models help basket makers plan their budget.
Grade 8 — Problem 2
Understanding sales trends helps forecast demand.
Makwa's basket sales are modeled by y = 3x + 10, where x is weeks and y is baskets sold. What is the slope, and what does it mean?
💡 Understanding sales trends helps forecast demand.
Grade 8 — Problem 3
Solving equations is critical when adapting traditional designs.
Solve for x: The total fiber length for a basket is 3x + 7 = 34 inches. How long is each section (x)?
💡 Solving for unknown dimensions is critical when adapting traditional designs.
Grade 8 — Problem 4
Coiled baskets exhibit exponential growth in their spiraling patterns.
A coiled basket's radius grows: coil 1 = 2cm, coil 2 = 4cm, coil 3 = 8cm. If this pattern continues, what is the radius at coil 5?
💡 Coiled baskets exhibit exponential growth in their spiraling patterns.
Grade 8 — Problem 5
Analyzing sales mix helps optimize production decisions.
Makwa sells small baskets for $15 and large baskets for $25. In one week she sold 20 baskets and earned $380. How many of each size did she sell? (Enter the number of small baskets.)
💡 Analyzing sales mix helps optimize which basket sizes to produce.
Grade 8 — Problem 6
Determining basket height from volume requirements ensures the design meets functional needs.
The volume of a cylindrical basket is V = πr²h. If r = 7 cm and the volume is 1,540 cm³, what is the height h? Use π ≈ 22/7.
💡 Determining basket height from volume requirements ensures the design meets functional needs.
