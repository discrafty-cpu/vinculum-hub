🪢 The Weaver's Secret: The axes on a coordinate grid work just like the warp threads on a loom. They're not abstract lines — they're reference threads that help you find exactly where to place each fiber. When a weaver says "go 3 across and 5 up," they're using coordinates!

💡 Weaver's Wisdom

🌿 The Warp Threads:
On a real loom, the warp threads run vertically (up-down). But here, we use them differently — the horizontal thread (x-axis) and vertical thread (y-axis) work together as reference guides. The x-axis tells you how far LEFT or RIGHT to go. The y-axis tells you how far UP or DOWN to go. Together, they help you find any spot on the loom!
