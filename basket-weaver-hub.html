Welcome to The Basket Weaver's Path

This is a demonstration prototype showing how indigenous basket weaving traditions can provide rich, culturally grounded contexts for K-12 mathematics instruction.

Important:
  • This is a concept demonstration, not a finished product
  • Cultural content is based on published research and requires review by indigenous educators before any classroom use
  • Mathematical problems are designed to meet grade-level standards
  • Traditional knowledge belongs to indigenous communities

Grounded in Ethnomathematics Research

This curriculum builds on decades of ethnomathematics scholarship demonstrating how mathematical thinking emerges from cultural practices:

Ron Eglash (Ethnomathematics) documents the deep mathematical structures in African fractal patterns and geometric systems.

David Barta & Bharath Sriraman explore how indigenous knowledge systems reveal sophisticated mathematical problem-solving embedded in craft traditions.

Ubiratan D'Ambrosio pioneered the field of ethnomathematics, showing how mathematics exists within cultural and social contexts across all human societies.

This work honors and centers indigenous mathematics while meeting rigorous grade-level standards.

The Learning Journey

🧺🧒 K–2
The Gathering Years | Nibi

Join Nibi as she learns to gather, sort, and count materials with Nokomis. Explore counting, addition, subtraction, shapes, and patterns through hands-on basket making.

Math Focus: Counting, basic operations, shapes, patterns, measurement
CRA Emphasis: 50%+ Concrete, learning through hands-on materials
18 interactive problems across K, 1st, 2nd grade
Start K-2 Journey →
🧶🧑 3–5
The Apprentice's Workshop | Makwa

Makwa begins designing baskets, learning fractions through color sections, multiplication through repeating patterns, and measurement through sizing.

Math Focus: Fractions, multiplication, area, perimeter, data analysis
CRA Emphasis: 35% Concrete, balanced representational and abstract
18 interactive problems across 3rd, 4th, 5th grade
Start 3-5 Journey →
📐🧑 6–8
The Master's Studio | Makwa

Makwa masters dye ratios, proportional scaling, and geometric transformations. Explore how algebra and geometry emerge naturally from advanced basket design.

Math Focus: Ratios, proportions, equations, geometry, statistics
CRA Emphasis: 20% Concrete, rising toward abstract thinking
18 interactive problems across 6th, 7th, 8th grade
Start 6-8 Journey →
📊🎓 9–12
The Innovator's Path | Ishkode

Ishkode brings basket traditions into the modern world—modeling curves with trigonometry, optimizing business with systems of equations, and analyzing patterns with statistics.

Math Focus: Functions, systems, trigonometry, statistics, calculus concepts
CRA Emphasis: 15% Concrete, majority abstract reasoning
24 interactive problems across 9th, 10th, 11th, 12th grade
Start 9-12 Journey →
🧶 The Fiber Loom
Interactive Coordinate Grid

Place fibers on a virtual weaving loom to discover how coordinates work. The warp threads ARE the axes — they're reference lines that help you find where each fiber goes. From counting grid points (K-2) to modeling parabolas (9-12).

Math Focus: Coordinates, ordered pairs, graphing, transformations, functions
CRA Emphasis: Concrete fiber placement → representational grid → abstract coordinate algebra
Interactive loom for all grade bands
