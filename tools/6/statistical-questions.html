Explore: Statistical vs Non-Statistical Questions

A statistical question is one that anticipates variability in the data. Click examples to learn the difference.

✓ Statistical Questions (Expect Varied Answers)

"How tall are students in Grade 6?" → Different students have different heights
"What are the daily temperatures in March?" → Temperatures vary day to day
"How many hours do students study per week?" → Students study different amounts

✗ Non-Statistical Questions (Single Answer)

"How tall is Sarah?" → One specific answer, no variability
"What is the boiling point of water?" → Always 100°C, no variation
"How many students are in our class?" → One fixed number
